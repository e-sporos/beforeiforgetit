I did what I always do. I paused. I went to my phone. Three hours later I was reading a digitised archive and I had completely forgotten what I originally searched for.

This is the part that gets me every time — how much ordinary life exists just underneath the surface of the historical record. The dates, the events, the famous names. And then below that: cafés, letters, favourite songs, small daily rituals nobody thought to preserve.

"The most interesting people are always the ones just out of frame."

What rabbit holes do to me is this: they start with a name on a screen and end with me feeling like I've sat across from someone who lived and breathed and was mostly forgotten. I don't think that's a small thing.

And so I write it down. Before I forget it.
